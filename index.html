About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The monument is admired worldwide for its beauty, symmetry, and emotional significance.

History

The Taj Mahal was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631. Construction began in 1632 and took about 20 years to complete. Thousands of skilled artisans and craftsmen worked to create this magnificent monument using white marble. Located in Agra, India, the Taj Mahal stands as a symbol of eternal love & devotion is admire for its beauty and architectural perfection. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World, attracting millions of visitors every year.

Architecture

The Taj Mahal is built using white marble and combines elements of Islamic, Persian, and Indian architecture. The central dome, minarets, gardens, and intricate carvings add to its breathtaking design.
